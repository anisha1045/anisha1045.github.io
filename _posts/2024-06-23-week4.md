Hello friends! After all of the coding and a day off Wednesday for Juneteenth, we finally got the robot to learn to stack cups on Thursday! During our last joint meeting with Elaine, she gave us different options to focus on for our individual projects, such as policy shaping, optimal robot movement, legibility and predictability, and making the robot seem more intelligent to the human. I thought it would be interesting to combine legibility and predictability with policy shaping, so I spent time looking into past work on both of them. There isn't much research on the intersection between them, though I did find a couple of papers on using reward shaping to make computer movements more predictable. Aside from research, I took a trip to South End for the rose garden and the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum with Emily and Larissa, my roommates. The rose garden was beautiful, as was the museum. I also was dying to try Blackbird Doughnuts, where I got a raspberry marshmallow donut that was quite toothsome. Next week, I will continue my exploration of Boston, look more into depth at past methods for research, and start coming up with how I might implement my ideas. Until next time!
