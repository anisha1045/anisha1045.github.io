Hey everyone, welcome back to my blog, and get ready to hear about Week 2 of my DREU! Last weekend, I went to North End in Boston for some cannolis, pizza, and Boston cream pie. After coming back to the lab on Monday, I read some studies on policy shaping, and there's  quite a bit of research on it using different types of human feedback (for example, investigating silence as feedback and incorrect feedback). However, I haven't yet found any research on using human feedback as a way to make the robot learn a slightly different policy than the one provided by the algorithm, and I think it would be an interesting study. Anyways, during our meeting with Dr. Short, she suggested that we start by writing a simple Q - learning algorithm on the robot arm to make sure we understand everything while she outlines the details on individual projects. Going through the tutorials last week wasn't too much fun, but now that I'm actually coding and have some more direction, I enjoy my time in the lab much more. So far, the program has been going well. Of course, we have run into some problems, but they were nothing a quick debugging session couldn't fix. Right now, it seems like the learning algorithm works, but getting the robot to move in conjunction is proving difficult. Hopefully we can get it to work in the next couple days, and we can get started on what comes next!
