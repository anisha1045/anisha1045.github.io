After that, my last few days were a blur, as I spent too much time last week running the tests and not enough time working on the final report. I got it done in time though, thank goodness. What I found after running the tests was that my algorithm worked as good as or better than the original for the vast majority of the tests, and that it worked drastically better with the grid size of 9x9. These results are quite useful because it could be used to explore real life environments, which tend to be large state spaces, possibly much faster and more legibly than the original algorithm.
